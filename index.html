Case Study

StreamVerse

Overview: StreamVerse is a streaming-style website concept designed to feel bold, cinematic, and entertainment-focused.

Challenge: The main challenge was organizing visual content in a way that felt exciting without becoming cluttered.

My Role: I worked on the layout, visual direction, HTML structure, CSS styling, and responsive behavior.

Process: I planned the page around a hero area, featured media cards, and clear calls-to-action. I used contrast, spacing, and image scale to create a streaming-platform feel.

Solution: The final design uses a dark interface, large imagery, strong headings, and interactive hover states to make the site feel modern and cinematic.

Results: The project helped me practice visual hierarchy, layout balance, and responsive design.

Reflection: I learned that entertainment websites need both visual energy and clean organization to keep users interested.

Case Study

FRAME Magazine

Overview: FRAME Magazine is a creative media website concept centered on photography, design, filmmaking, and visual storytelling.

Challenge: The challenge was making multiple content types feel unified within one design system.

My Role: I developed the page structure, typography, content sections, multimedia elements, and responsive styling.

Process: I explored magazine-style layouts, article cards, media sections, and repeated design patterns to make the site feel like a real publication.

Solution: The final design uses card-based content, section headings, media previews, and strong spacing to create a polished editorial experience.

Results: This project strengthened my understanding of consistency, reusable CSS, and storytelling through page layout.

Reflection: I learned that a strong brand system helps large websites feel organized and professional.

Case Study

The Filmmaker

Overview: The Filmmaker is a production planning project that connects creative storytelling with practical filmmaking decisions.

Challenge: The challenge was evaluating whether a creative idea could work within real production limits.

My Role: I analyzed the project concept, considered locations, reviewed production needs, and organized the final presentation.

Process: I broke the project into key planning areas such as setting, visuals, risks, schedule, and production feasibility.

Solution: The final project presented a clearer plan for how the film could be approached, produced, and communicated.

Results: The project showed my ability to think beyond the idea and consider what it takes to execute a creative vision.

Reflection: I learned that strong storytelling depends on planning, organization, and realistic decision-making.
